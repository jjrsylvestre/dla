§8.7 # 8.104: The symbol \({}\cap{}\) means "intersection of...." So, in this question, \(U \cap W\) means "the collection of all vectors that are simultaneously part of both collections, \(U\) and \(W\)." An intersection of subspaces is always a subspace, because they share the zero vector (and so are non-empty), and they are both closed under the vector operations (see problem # 7.59).
